Dmitri Mendeleev

Father of the Periodic Table

A brief timeline:

  • 1834 - Born in Verkhnie Aremzyani, Russia
  • 1849 - Applied to Moscow University, but was rejected
  • 1850 - Entered the Main Pedagogical Institute
  • 1861 - Published a textbook called Organic Chemistry, which would go on to win the Demidov Prize of the Petersburg Academy of Sciences
  • 1867 - Discovered that elements could be classified by their chemical properites, began work on a new periodic table
  • 1869 - Presented his findings to the Russian Chemical Society. Also correctly predicted the properties of several elements that were undiscovered at the time
  • 1905 - Elected a member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences
  • 1907 - Died at 72 in Saint Petersburg, Russia
